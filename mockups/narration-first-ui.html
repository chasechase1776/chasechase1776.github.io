Annual Plan

Big-picture school-year planning

The Annual Plan documents intent: theme, curriculum spines, weekly rhythm, unit-study arc, journals, capstones, and annual records. Daily logs remain the record of what actually happened.

Section 3

Weekly Rhythm

Flexible planning scaffold

Each week includes a writing prompt developed throughout the week and finalized on Friday. Friday is the weekly culmination point; at the end of a unit, Final Friday becomes a larger unit capstone.

Question MondayIntroduce weekly question, science topic, writing topic, and project direction. Evidence: discussion note, first sketch, prompt draft.
Exploration TuesdayHands-on exploration, nature, music, observation, experimentation, and continued core work. Evidence: journal page, photo, experiment note.
Context WednesdayHistory, geography, maps, biographies, timelines, unit reading, and continued core work. Evidence: map, timeline, narration.
Meaning ThursdayCitizenship, philosophy, emotional intelligence, responsibility, service, communication, social studies, and project work. Evidence: reflection, discussion note.
Creating FridayFinalize writing, present, complete project, art, science experiment, portfolio artifact, reflection, optional notable person review or Thinker Toy.
Final Friday SummaryUnit capstone: present the final project, explain learning, and select proof-of-learning artifacts.

Section 4

Unit Study Format Options

Harbor & Sprout TemplateUse the Monday-Friday pattern: science/writing, nature/music, history/geography, U.S. study/citizenship/philosophy/EQ, and Friday presentation/art/science.
Open-and-Go Published UnitFollow the publisher sequence in general. The app still tags activities, subjects, legal categories, skills, artifacts, and time.
Minimal Structure / Parent-DesignedUse the weekly rhythm as the default scaffold for parent-created activities, projects, writing prompts, presentation goals, and artifacts.
Planning noteThese fields are the intended framework. The parent can deviate based on interest, pacing, field trips, family schedule, or unit depth.

Snapshot/export concept

Markdown records generated from database records

Database remains the source of truth. Markdown is a generated snapshot layer for readable archives, backups, long-term records, and Obsidian-style browsing. Manual Markdown edits do not update the database unless a future import/sync feature is intentionally added.
